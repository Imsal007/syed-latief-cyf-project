London: The Majestic Capital on the Thames

With over two millennia of history, London is a vibrant metropolis where old-world majesty meets modern innovation. From its red double-decker buses to its grand royal palaces, every street corner tells a captivating story of the past and present.

1. The Historic District of Westminster

Situated in the heart of London, the City of Westminster is the political and cultural epicentre of the nation. It is home to grand architecture, royal traditions, and some of the world's most famous landmarks.

If you visit Westminster, you must explore these three incredible sites:

  • The royal sanctuary of Westminster Abbey
  • The scenic walk across Westminster Bridge
  • The sovereign home at Buckingham Palace

The Palace of Westminster

Commonly known as the Houses of Parliament, this stunning Gothic revival palace sits gracefully on the banks of the River Thames. It serves as the meeting place for the government and stands as a global symbol of democracy.

The Elizabeth Tower

Rising majestically at the north end of the palace is the striking 96-metre-tall Elizabeth Tower. Completed in 1859, its four massive clock faces have reliably kept time for Londoners through world wars, historic events, and changing eras.

The Great Bell Called Big Ben

While many people call the entire tower "Big Ben," that famous nickname actually belongs strictly to the colossal, 13.7-tonne iron bell hanging deep inside the clockwork framework. Its deep, resonant chimes echo across the city every single hour.

The Precise Stacks of Old Pennies

Fun Fact: The clock’s incredible accuracy is adjusted using a surprisingly simple method. By adding or removing a single old British penny coin on top of the giant swinging pendulum, the keepers can speed up or slow down the clock by a tiny fraction of a second per day!
